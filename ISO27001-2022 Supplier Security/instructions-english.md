# Supplier Cybersecurity Self-Assessment Instructions

## üìã Purpose of This Assessment

This self-assessment form has been designed to evaluate your organization's information security maturity level as part of our supply chain risk management program. 

The assessment is based on **ISO/IEC 27001:2022** - the international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). By completing this assessment, you help us:

- **Understand your security posture** and identify potential risks in our supply chain
- **Ensure compliance** with information security requirements
- **Build trust** through transparency about security practices
- **Identify areas for collaboration** and mutual improvement

This is not an audit or certification process. It's a practical evaluation tool to understand how you manage information security risks.

---

## üìù How to Fill Out the Assessment

### Step 1: Open the Form
Open the HTML file (`supplier-cybersecurity-assessment.html`) in any modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari).

### Step 2: Complete Company Information
Fill in your basic company details:
- Company name
- Contact person name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Assessment date (auto-filled with today's date)
- Number of employees (optional)

### Step 3: Answer All Questions
The assessment contains **39 questions** organized into **10 sections**:

1. **Organizational Controls & Policies** - Your security governance and policies
2. **Risk Management & Assessment** - How you identify and manage security risks
3. **People Controls & Awareness** - Employee security training and responsibilities
4. **Access Control & Identity Management** - Who can access what information
5. **Physical & Environmental Security** - Protection of physical assets
6. **Technical & Technological Controls** - Cybersecurity tools and protections
7. **Backup & Business Continuity** - Data protection and disaster recovery
8. **Incident Management & Response** - How you handle security incidents
9. **Supplier & Third-Party Management** - Security in your supply chain
10. **Compliance, Audit & Continuous Improvement** - Monitoring and improvement

For each question, select the maturity level from the dropdown menu that best describes your current state.

### Step 4: Track Your Progress
The progress bar at the top shows how many questions you've answered. Aim for 100% completion.

### Step 5: Add Additional Information
At the end, you can:
- List any security certifications you hold (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)
- Provide additional comments about your security practices

## üéØ Understanding Maturity Levels

Choose the option that most accurately reflects your organization's current practices:

| Maturity Level | Description | When to Select |
|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Not Implemented** | No formal process or control exists | You don't have this control in place at all |
| **Partially Implemented** | Process exists but is informal, inconsistent, or incomplete | You do this sometimes or informally, but it's not documented or consistent |
| **Implemented** | Process is documented and consistently followed | You have documented procedures that are regularly followed |
| **Fully Mature** | Process is optimized, regularly reviewed, and continuously improved | You not only follow the process but regularly measure, review, and improve it |
| **Not Applicable** | Control is not relevant to your organization | This doesn't apply to your business model or operations |

**Important Tips:**
- ‚úÖ Be honest - this helps both organizations
- ‚úÖ If unsure between two levels, choose the lower one
- ‚úÖ "Not Applicable" is a valid answer for some controls
- ‚úÖ You can save partially completed forms and continue later

## üíæ Saving Your Assessment

### Save and Review
1. Click the **"Save Assessment to Disk"** button at the bottom of the form
2. The file will download to your computer with your answers saved
3. The filename will include your company name and the date
4. You can reopen this saved file at any time to:
   - Review your answers
   - Make changes
   - Complete any missed questions

### Your Maturity Score
When you save, you'll see:
- **Maturity Score** (0-3 scale) - your overall security maturity
- **Maturity Level Classification**:
  - **Initial** (0-0.5): Significant improvements needed
  - **Developing** (0.5-1.5): Basic security practices in place
  - **Defined** (1.5-2.5): Good security practices established
  - **Optimized** (2.5-3.0): Mature security practices

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does it take to complete?**
A: Typically 15-25 minutes, depending on your organization's size and complexity.

**Q: Can I save and complete it later?**
A: Yes! Click "Save Assessment to Disk" at any time. You can reopen the file and continue where you left off.

**Q: What if I don't know the answer to a question?**
A: Consult with relevant team members (IT, security, compliance). If truly unknown, be honest in your response or select the most conservative option.

**Q: Will this affect our business relationship?**
A: This assessment helps us work together more effectively. Low scores don't automatically disqualify suppliers - they help identify where we can support each other.

**Q: Can I update my assessment later?**
A: Yes, we typically request updates annually or when significant changes occur in your security practices.

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: The assessment file is saved locally on your computer. You control when and how to share it.
