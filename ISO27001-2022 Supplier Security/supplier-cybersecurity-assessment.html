Supplier Cybersecurity Self-Assessment

Based on ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Information Security Management System Requirements

Please complete all sections to help us understand your information security maturity level

Assessment Progress: 0%

Supplier Information

Maturity Level Definitions

Not Implemented: No formal process or control exists
Partially Implemented: Process exists but is informal, inconsistent, or incomplete
Implemented: Process is documented and consistently followed
Fully Mature: Process is optimized, regularly reviewed, and continuously improved
Not Applicable: Control is not relevant to your organization

1. Organizational Controls & Policies

1.1 Do you have a documented information security policy approved by management?
1.2 Are information security roles and responsibilities clearly defined and assigned?
1.3 Do you maintain an inventory of information assets (hardware, software, data)?
1.4 Do you have procedures for information classification and handling?

2. Risk Management & Assessment

2.1 Do you conduct regular information security risk assessments?
2.2 Do you have a documented risk treatment plan with assigned owners?
2.3 Do you monitor and evaluate security threats and vulnerabilities relevant to your business?

3. People Controls & Awareness

3.1 Do you conduct background screening for employees with access to sensitive information?
3.2 Do employees receive regular information security awareness training?
3.3 Do employees sign confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements?
3.4 Are there procedures for employee termination including access revocation?

4. Access Control & Identity Management

4.1 Do you have documented access control policies based on business needs?
4.2 Are user access rights reviewed regularly and removed when no longer needed?
4.3 Do you enforce strong password policies or multi-factor authentication?
4.4 Is privileged access (administrative rights) restricted and monitored?

5. Physical & Environmental Security

5.1 Are physical security perimeters defined to protect sensitive areas?
5.2 Do you have access controls for physical entry to offices and facilities?
5.3 Are clear desk and clear screen policies enforced?
5.4 Do you have secure disposal procedures for equipment and media containing sensitive data?

6. Technical & Technological Controls

6.1 Do you use anti-malware protection on all systems and keep it updated?
6.2 Are software patches and security updates applied regularly?
6.3 Do you use encryption to protect sensitive data in transit and at rest?
6.4 Are network security controls implemented (firewalls, network segmentation)?
6.5 Do you maintain system logs and monitor them for security events?

7. Backup & Business Continuity

7.1 Do you perform regular backups of critical data and systems?
7.2 Are backup restoration procedures tested regularly?
7.3 Do you have a documented business continuity plan for information security?
7.4 Is the business continuity plan tested at regular intervals?

8. Incident Management & Response

8.1 Do you have documented procedures for reporting security incidents?
8.2 Do you have an incident response plan with defined roles and procedures?
8.3 Do you maintain records of security incidents and lessons learned?
8.4 Do you have procedures for evidence collection and preservation for incidents?

9. Supplier & Third-Party Management

9.1 Do you assess information security risks when engaging suppliers or third parties?
9.2 Do your supplier agreements include information security requirements?
9.3 Do you monitor and review third-party security practices regularly?

10. Compliance, Audit & Continuous Improvement

10.1 Are you aware of and compliant with relevant legal and regulatory requirements?
10.2 Do you conduct internal security audits or assessments regularly?
10.3 Is there a management review process for information security performance?
10.4 Do you have a process for continual improvement of security controls?
